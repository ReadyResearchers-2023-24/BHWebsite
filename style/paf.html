The Pan-African Flag

1900

All white minstrel group singers were heavly talking about the importance of a flag designed to respresent ones race. Will A. Heelan and J. Fred Helf created a song titled Every Race Has A Flag But the Coon

July 20, 1914

The UNIA was founded by Marcus Garvey when he was only 28 years old. The association was very big on Black pride, redeeming Africa from the rule of white athority, and unity within the African American community.

The meaning of this flag is to symbolize unity of Black people as a nation despite slavery.

1920

Marcus Gavery, along with members of the UNIA, created the Pan-Arican flag using the colors red, black, and green. This flag was made in response to Every Race Has a Flag but the Coon" a folk song made to mock Black people.

Symbols of Solidarity

Red: Blood

The red within the flag represents the connection of all people of African ancestry and the blood that was shed for liberation

Black: Nation - Not State

The black does not represent the people as a state because that is unknown for sure. Therefore black represents the people as a nation. Green: Land The green represents the great and natural wealth of Africa and its land African countries such as Kenya, Malawi, and Libya share the same color scheme

Green: Land

The green represents the great and natural wealth of Africa and its land
